# Supplier Guidelines

## Supplier rating methodology

Each supplier carries a rating between 0 and 1, reviewed quarterly. The rating
blends:

- On-time delivery percentage (weight 50%)
- Quality acceptance rate at goods inward (weight 30%)
- Commercial responsiveness — quotes, confirmations, documentation (weight 20%)

Ratings of 0.90+ are considered excellent, 0.75–0.89 good, 0.60–0.74 acceptable,
and below 0.60 not eligible for automatic recommendation.

## Delivery performance monitoring

The supplier scorecard tracks, per supplier, over delivered POs:

- **Total delivered orders**
- **Average delivery variance** in days — actual minus promised delivery date;
  negative values mean early delivery.
- **On-time percentage** — share of orders delivered on or before the promised
  date. Target is 85% or better.

A supplier whose on-time percentage falls below 70% for two consecutive quarters
must be put on a corrective action plan.

## Blacklisting criteria

A supplier is suspended from new orders when any of the following occurs:

- On-time percentage below 50% over the last 10 orders.
- Two consecutive quality rejections of the same material.
- Rating falls below 0.40.

Suspension is lifted only after a successful trial order approved by the
procurement manager.

## Onboarding a new supplier

1. Commercial and quality audit questionnaire.
2. Trial order of no more than the material's EOQ.
3. Rating initialized at 0.70 and adjusted after the first five deliveries.
4. Mapping to materials with agreed unit price, lead time and minimum order
   quantity recorded in the supplier–material mapping.

## Lead time commitments

The lead time recorded per supplier–material mapping is a contractual promise.
The ML lead-time model predicts the **actual** expected lead time from history;
when the predicted lead time exceeds the promised lead time by more than 3 days,
planners should use the predicted value and raise the gap with the supplier.
